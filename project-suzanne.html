The process of Suzanne Collins site redesign

Conceptualized and designed a content hub for Suzanne Collins fans. Following the steady decrease of demand for Hunger Games books and merchandise in the web, Suzanne Collins needs to have a compelling web experience for her audience.

My role

  • Researcher
  • Information Architect
  • UX Designer

1. Project Kick-off

Suzanne Collins’s website is widely perceived as incomplete, buggy and confusing. It seems oddly out of sync with what we come to expect from such a successful and influential writer. To change this perception, the client wants the website to become a safe harbor for Suzanne’s audience. It needs to be perceived as an active channel and also as an useful hub for all things related to Suzanne Collins.

2. Choosing my focus

The majority of readers come from the Hunger Games series. So with that in mind, our goal is to tap into that particular interest and increase awareness about other aspects of Suzanne’s work, such as existing and future projects, interviews, events and reviews. Our goal is to create a community of readers interested in her work.

3. Early testing with IA models

By seeing what other writers were doing, I could start imagining the steps we would need to take in order to improve the website and engage with our audience.

4. Information Architecture

I created organizational schemas and learned what content it was available in order to start structuring flows.

5. Sketching

Using Adobe XD, I was able to prototype low-fi options. I explored layout ideas and tested with users. Their feedback resulted in the live website that was coded by me here
