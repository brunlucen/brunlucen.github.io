Customer Success and the logic of experience

Developed, negotiated and implemented Customer Success strategies that are managing to increase client retention and increase cross-sale initiatives.

1. Project Kick-off

We started the project by having a meeting with the stakeholders. This project was a continuation of another that mapped all the pipelines and flows. So we participated in this handover and set goals and initial planning.

2. Exploration and analysis

With a briefing in hands, we started exploring the processes and concepts we were dealing with. The first one was the logic of experience. We mapped experience as a result of user expectation and user perception. Those are the primary factors that define an experience from the point of view of the user.

3. Conceptual models

One of the big wins of this project was to introduce the idea of "making the user better at his tasks". We devised a plan to get the user from newbie to master. We hoped that having this as a guiding force, we'd be better suited to create value for the user.

Bruno is a flexible person, used to share and exchange knowledge, working in a centralised and actualised way. I enjoyed working with him in this project.

- Barbara Werneck

4. Game thinking

We drew the model from Amy Jo Kim's Game thinking. We tested with a few power users.

5. Service Blueprinting

When we reached service blueprint phase, we had a good idea of what was happening in the phases of activation and onboarding. We proposed some big improvements that resulted in a completely new activation flow and an onboarding process.

6. Results and next steps

After implementation, we continued to test our solutions. The work is absolutely enormous so we had to focus on a few key steps. I'll write more of this in the blog. Thank you!
